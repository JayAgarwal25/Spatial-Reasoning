Contribution Statement — Quant-EpiGNN
CS F425 Deep Learning, BITS Pilani
Team 7

All members read Spatial457 (Wang et al., CVPR 2025), PRISM-0 (Elskhawy et al., 2025),
VILASR (Wu et al., 2025), the Epistemic GNN paper (Ergün & Bhatt, ICLR 2025), and
"What's Up with Vision-Language Models?" (Kamath et al., EMNLP 2023) as shared
background. Step-specific papers were divided as noted below.

Jay Agarwal (2022B5A71305P)
Additionally read the Neural Message Passing paper (Gilmer et al., ICML 2017) as
background for the GNN design. Responsible for overall system integration and took
ownership of the training pipeline. Implemented the Quant-EpiGNN (Step 2) including
the epistemic node states, geometric consistency residuals, and residual-derived edge
weights for message passing. Ran all training and ablation experiments on
Spatial457-20k. Wrote the methodology and results sections of the report, and
prepared the live demo.

Gorang Mehrishi (2022B4A70807P)
Additionally read Grounding DINO (Liu et al., 2023) as background for open-vocabulary
object detection in Step 1. Implemented the full scene graph extraction pipeline —
object detection, depth estimation, geometric pair pruning, and relation extraction.
Helped with dataset preparation and contributed to the related work section of the
report.

Mayukh Khetan (2022B5A71326P)
Additionally read Qwen2-VL (Wang et al., 2024) in depth as the baseline model used
for spatial distance prediction. Implemented the evaluation pipeline (Step 4)
including the dataset loader, metrics (MAE, MRA, triangle-inequality violation rate),
and the Qwen2-VL-7B baseline integration. Wrote the evaluation and baselines sections
of the report.

Harsimar Singh Saluja (2022A7PS1187P)
Additionally read ReAct (Yao et al., 2022) as the basis for the iterative
reasoning-action loop design in Step 3. Implemented the residual-based trigger, image
annotation actions (draw_bbox, draw_line, depth crop), and the iterative visual
grounding loop. Wrote tests for the Step 3 pipeline and contributed to the
introduction and related work sections of the report.
